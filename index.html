• About

    This is a small personal design project for an ear training tool using solfège. It was conceived as an exercise in interface design as well as an introduction to newer HTML5/CSS developments.


    I attempted to accomplish this project relatively quickly with as little JavaScript as I could get away with. As a result, most of the effects you see are heavily implemented via CSS with little consideration given to browser compatibility or progressive enhancement. My use of jQuery is largely limited to handling the tone generation and toggling classes.


    Since I am not primarily a front-end developer (if you couldn't tell already), I thought it would be a good opportunity to round out my skillset.


    Right now, it is still a work in progress. I am currently planning on:


    • Fixing jQuery event handling on the radial menu for click events instead of hovering.
    • A game loop to continuously generate new scenarios ad infinitum without refreshing.
    • Configurable solfege pronunciations.
    • Configurable octaves and keys.
    • More bulletproof markup and styling.
    • Design support for different resolutions and devices, including phones and tablets.
  • How to Use

    Simply press the icon in the center of the screen to play a tone and activate the radial menu. Hover over the name of the tone you think it is, and the ring will turn green if you are correct, red if not.


    NOTE: A new scenario will be generated automatically after you guess a tone correctly. The tones are currently limited to a single octave of the C Major scale based on a tonic of C4 (Middle C), although C5 is missing.
